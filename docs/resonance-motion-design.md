# Resonance motion design

## Brand motion language

héReSonare motion follows a four-part vocabulary. Every prominent animation
must express at least one of these meanings instead of adding movement only for
decoration.

| Motion | Meaning | Primary use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pulse | Sound is triggered | Buttons, logo, and navigation feedback |
| Ripple | Resonance propagates outwards | Hero, cards, and future transitions |
| Orbit | Creativity and a future-facing system | Light nodes, particles, and ambient backgrounds |
| Converge | Different voices form a connection | Contact, footer, and page endings |

The homepage opens with the existing spectrum as its sound source. Interaction
then activates light on the controls the visitor is actually approaching,
rather than inserting a second brand mark into the release card. Ripple and
Orbit remain available for ambient backgrounds and approved media compositions.
The final Contact and Footer phase will use Converge to bring separate paths
back to one contact point.

## Adaptation policy

Motion examples are references for interaction principles only. The site does
not copy their composition, source, assets, color system, or product-specific
behavior. Components, timing, geometry, and responsive behavior are designed
for the héReSonare identity and existing content architecture.

The current reference mechanisms are physical stagger, scroll-linked text,
spring pointer response, path drawing, and restrained card tilt. Premium
Motion+ source is not required or copied.

## Timing and behavior

- Echo delays stay in the 60–90 ms range so propagation is visible as one
  connected event.
- Pulse and Ripple use decisive attack and slower decay rather than bounce.
- Orbit is slower than content motion and never communicates loading.
- Pointer response is limited to fine pointers. Touch layouts do not tilt or
  chase touch coordinates.
- Scroll-linked movement uses Motion values and transforms without React state
  updates on every frame.
- Reduced Motion removes propagation, orbit, pointer travel, and scroll-linked
  offsets. All content remains present and readable.

## Delivery phases

1. Homepage identity: pointer-responsive button light and localized resonance ticker.
2. Global interaction: tiered light response for controls, cards, information
   panels, navigation, and inner-page headings.
3. Brand story: connected About path and Contact/Footer Converge accents.
   Implemented with scroll-drawn traces and restrained convergence targets.
4. Media pages: Productions now uses code-native Signal Media Frames and a
   gated image-reveal path. Artist portraits, release covers, video thumbnails,
   coverflow, and image parallax remain deferred until approved media exists.
